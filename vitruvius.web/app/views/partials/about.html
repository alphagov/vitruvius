Vitruvius

Vitruvius is a prototype register of Government platforms, services, components and registers

Platform

We define a platform as a unit of government behaviour common to and consumed by multiple services. It is run independently from a service and will have a service manager who coordinates it's stakeholders in terms of when and what functional changes can be made.

See here for a list of platforms

Service

We define a service as a unit of government behaviour important enough to have a service manager permanently and probably uniquely assigned to it. In a sense it is a ‘business’ Service - it encompasses people, process and technology - but following the ‘digital by default’ policy.

See here for a list of services

Component

A component is a piece of useful code, content, data or tooling that can be deployed in many services. There may be many implementations of the same component, in many languages. Components encourage reuse of assets across government projects. Components should be versioned to allow ease of use by other services. Component owners and users should be aware of changes to the components and the impact of change.

See here for a list of components

Register

Registers allow access to a data store via an API. Registers may not allow direct access to the data but rather an appropriate subset of data held to a service. Requests for data should always be recorded along with responses. To use a register you will need to be approved and issued with appropriate authentication.

See here for a list of registers
